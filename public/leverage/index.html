Why Leverage Changes Everything

In 1665, Isaac Newton discovered that a small force at the right point could move the world. This principle of leverage extends far beyond physics. It is the fundamental law of achievement.

Consider the greatest empires. They were built by those who worked smartest, not hardest. Alexander leveraged strategy. The Medici leveraged finance. Jobs leveraged design. Each found the right leverage points.

In your life, leverage appears in many forms:

Time: Systems that work while you sleep
Knowledge: Skills that compound over decades
Network: Relationships that open doors
Capital: Money that generates more money
Technology: Tools that amplify capabilities

Reflection: Where could you apply more leverage for 10x results?

This journal teaches you to identify and create leverage. Each practice builds momentum. Each reflection deepens thinking. Each goal becomes a lever for the next level.
